DIAGNOSIS: Obstructive Sleep Apnea, Moderate; Insomnia

CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Patient referred by primary care physician for evaluation of chronic fatigue, snoring,
and poor sleep quality. Wife reports loud snoring and witnessed apneas during sleep.

SLEEP HISTORY:
Patient reports difficulty falling asleep (takes 45-60 minutes), frequent awakenings
throughout night (3-5 times), early morning awakening (4:30-5:00 AM), non-restorative sleep.
Reports being tired all day despite spending 7-8 hours in bed.

Contributing factors:
- Chronic back pain (wakes due to pain)
- PTSD symptoms (nightmares, hypervigilance)
- Stress and anxiety

POLYSOMNOGRAPHY RESULTS (October 28, 2025):

Sleep Architecture:
- Total sleep time: 4.2 hours (out of 7 hours in bed)
- Sleep efficiency: 60% (normal >85%)
- Sleep latency: 52 minutes (prolonged)
- REM latency: 145 minutes
- Stage N1: 28% (elevated, normal 5-10%)
- Stage N2: 48%
- Stage N3: 12% (reduced, normal 15-20%)
- Stage REM: 12% (reduced, normal 20-25%)

Respiratory Events:
- Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI): 22 events/hour (MODERATE OSA)
  * Normal: <5
  * Mild: 5-15
  * Moderate: 15-30
  * Severe: >30
- Oxygen desaturation index: 19 events/hour

Position: Supine AHI 35, Side AHI 15 (positional component)

INTERPRETATION:
Study demonstrates moderate obstructive sleep apnea with significant sleep fragmentation.
Multiple factors contributing to poor sleep quality:
1. Obstructive sleep apnea (AHI 22)
2. Chronic pain-related awakenings
3. PTSD-related sleep disturbance (nightmares, hypervigilance)
4. Insomnia with prolonged sleep latency

ASSESSMENT:
1. Obstructive Sleep Apnea, Moderate (G47.33)
2. Chronic Insomnia Disorder (F51.01)
3. Sleep disturbance secondary to chronic pain
4. Sleep disturbance secondary to PTSD

PLAN:
1. CPAP therapy initiated - Auto-CPAP with pressure range 6-16 cm H2O
2. Sleep hygiene education
3. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I) referral
4. Continue prazosin for PTSD-related nightmares
5. Pain management optimization for better sleep
6. Follow-up in 4 weeks for CPAP compliance check

NEXUS OPINION:
Patient's sleep apnea is likely aggravated by service-connected conditions:
- PTSD contributes to insomnia and sleep fragmentation
- Chronic pain from back condition causes frequent awakenings
- Weight gain related to decreased physical activity from service-connected disabilities
  may contribute to OSA severity

While sleep apnea etiology is multifactorial, service-connected conditions are at least as
likely as not (>50% probability) to be significant contributing factors to the severity
and persistence of this sleep disorder.

FUNCTIONAL IMPACT:
- Excessive daytime sleepiness (Epworth Sleepiness Scale: 16/24 - moderate to severe)
- Difficulty concentrating at work
- Safety concerns (patient reports near-miss while driving due to drowsiness)
- Worsens PTSD symptoms (sleep deprivation increases anxiety, irritability)
- Worsens pain perception (poor sleep lowers pain threshold)

RECOMMENDATIONS FOR VA:
Consider service connection for sleep apnea secondary to:
- PTSD (38 CFR 3.310(a))
- Chronic pain/back condition (38 CFR 3.310(a))

If service-connected, likely rating under DC 6847:
- 30%: Chronic respiratory failure with cor pulmonale, or; requires use of breathing
  assistance device (CPAP)
- 50%: Chronic respiratory failure with carbon dioxide retention or cor pulmonale, or;
  requires tracheostomy

Patient requires CPAP nightly, supporting 50% rating consideration.
