DATE OF EXAM: September 12, 2025
EXAMINER: Dr. Sarah Thompson, MD
CONDITION: Degenerative Disc Disease, Lumbar Spine

CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Veteran reports constant lower back pain since service-related injury in 2018. Pain is described
as sharp and radiating, rated 6-7/10 on most days, increasing to 8-9/10 with physical activity.

HISTORY:
Veteran injured lower back during training exercise in 2018 while on active duty. Initial treatment
included physical therapy and NSAIDs. MRI from 2019 showed disc bulging at L4-L5 and L5-S1.
Pain has progressively worsened since separation from service.

CURRENT SYMPTOMS:
- Constant lower back pain, worse with standing, walking, lifting
- Pain radiates down left leg to knee (possible radiculopathy)
- Stiffness in morning lasting 1-2 hours
- Difficulty sleeping due to pain (wakes 3-4 times per night)
- Uses ibuprofen 800mg 3x daily
- Unable to perform yard work, heavy lifting, or prolonged physical activities
- Ambulates with slight antalgic gait

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:
Range of Motion (measured with goniometer):
- Forward flexion: 60 degrees (normal: 90 degrees) - PAINFUL

- Left lateral flexion: 20 degrees (normal: 30 degrees) - PAINFUL
- Right lateral flexion: 20 degrees (normal: 30 degrees) - PAINFUL
- Left lateral rotation: 20 degrees (normal: 30 degrees)
- Right lateral rotation: 20 degrees (normal: 30 degrees)

Neurological:

IMAGING REVIEW:
MRI Lumbar Spine (May 2025):
- Disc bulge at L4-L5 with mild central canal stenosis
- Disc bulge at L5-S1 with left foraminal narrowing
- Facet arthropathy at multiple levels
- No evidence of nerve root compression

FUNCTIONAL IMPACT:
Veteran reports significant impact on daily activities:
- Cannot work full 8-hour days (reduced to 6 hours)
- Unable to play with children
- Difficulty with household chores
- Stopped recreational activities (hiking, sports)
- Requires frequent position changes

CURRENT TREATMENT:
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen 800mg TID)
- Physical therapy 2x per month
- Heating pad for pain relief
- Considering epidural steroid injections

EXAMINER'S OPINION:
Veteran has significant degenerative disc disease of the lumbar spine with radiculopathy.
Current symptoms and objective findings suggest chronic condition with functional limitations.

Based on 38 CFR 4.71a Schedule of Ratings – Spine:
- 5243 Intervertebral disc syndrome
  * 20%: Forward flexion of the thoracolumbar spine greater than 30 degrees but not greater
    than 60 degrees with painful motion
  * 40%: Forward flexion of the thoracolumbar spine 30 degrees or less with painful motion

EXAMINER RECOMMENDATION:
Current 20% rating appears consistent with examination findings. However, veteran's symptoms
including radiculopathy (radiating pain, sensory changes, positive nerve tension signs) may
warrant separate rating consideration under DC 8520 (incomplete paralysis of sciatic nerve).

Additional consideration for sleep disturbance as secondary to chronic pain.
